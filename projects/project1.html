Overview

"Chinese New Year without Borders" is a project that explores the cultural significance of Chinese New Year celebrations across different regions and communities around the world. This project aimed to create a visual storytelling experience that captures the universal joy and traditions of the festival while highlighting the unique regional variations.

The project received the Yang Yu-Long Jury Award at the 5th Taiwan Creative Star Design Award, recognizing its innovative approach to cultural expression through motion graphics and visual design.

The Challenge

The main challenge was to create a visual language that could represent the diversity of Chinese New Year celebrations while maintaining a cohesive narrative. The design needed to be accessible to audiences from different cultural backgrounds while staying authentic to the traditions being represented.

The Process

I began with extensive research on Chinese New Year traditions across different regions including mainland China, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and diaspora communities worldwide. This research phase involved collecting visual references, studying traditional symbolism, and understanding the cultural nuances of each celebration.

The design process involved creating a series of motion graphics that captured key elements of the celebration, including:

  • Traditional decorations and symbols
  • Family gathering and reunion dinner
  • Regional food specialties
  • Local customs and rituals
  • Modern adaptations of traditional practices

The Outcome

The final project featured a series of animated sequences that seamlessly transitioned between different cultural interpretations of Chinese New Year. By using a consistent color palette dominated by traditional red and gold while incorporating regional motifs, the design achieved a balance between unity and diversity.

The project was presented as an interactive digital exhibition and later adapted for social media platforms to reach a wider audience. The positive reception culminated in the prestigious Yang Yu-Long Jury Award at the Taiwan Creative Star Design Award.

Reflection

This project reinforced the importance of thorough research when working with cultural themes. It also highlighted how motion graphics can transcend language barriers to communicate shared human experiences while respecting cultural specificity.

The challenge of balancing tradition with contemporary design elements proved to be a valuable learning experience that continues to influence my approach to culturally-informed design work.
